## Syllabus Overview

### Paper I (250 marks)
**Part A: Political Theory**
- Concepts: State, Power, Authority, Justice, Rights, Democracy
- Western Thinkers: Plato to Rawls
- Indian Political Thought: Ancient to Modern

**Part B: Indian Government & Politics**
- Nationalist Movement
- Constitution Making
- Federal System
- Political Processes
- Social Movements

### Paper II (250 marks)
**Part A: Comparative Politics**
- Political Systems (UK, USA, China, etc.)
- Comparative Analysis
- Political Economy

**Part B: International Relations**
- IR Theories
- India's Foreign Policy
- Global Issues
- International Organizations

## Comprehensive Booklist

### Paper I - Part A (Political Theory)

**Core Books:**
1. **O.P. Gauba** - "Political Theory"
   - Best for beginners
   - Covers entire syllabus
   - Easy language

2. **Andrew Heywood** - "Political Theory: An Introduction"
   - In-depth coverage
   - Multiple perspectives
   - Advanced concepts

**Supplementary:**
- **IGNOU Material** (BA Political Science)
- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy** (Online - for thinkers)

### Paper I - Part B (Indian Politics)

**Core Books:**
1. **M. Laxmikanth** - "Indian Polity"
   - Constitutional aspects
   - Government structure
   - Must-read

2. **Bipan Chandra** - "India's Struggle for Independence"
   - Freedom movement
   - Nationalist politics
   - Constitutional development

3. **D.D. Basu** - "Introduction to Constitution of India"
   - Constitutional provisions
   - Case laws
   - Amendments

4. **Rajni Kothari** - "Politics in India"
   - Party system
   - Political processes
   - Social movements

**Supplementary:**
- **NCERT** - Politics in India (Class 12)
- **Ramachandra Guha** - India After Gandhi

### Paper II - Part A (Comparative Politics)

**Core Books:**
1. **Rod Hague & Martin Harrop** - "Comparative Government and Politics"
   - Best comprehensive book
   - Covers all countries
   - Updated content

2. **IGNOU Material** - MPS-003 (Comparative Politics)
   - Structured content
   - Examination focused

### Paper II - Part B (International Relations)

**Core Books:**
1. **Pushpesh Pant** - "International Relations"
   - India-focused
   - Contemporary issues

2. **V.N. Khanna** - "International Relations"

## Study Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (3 months)

**Goals:**
- Complete one reading of all core books
- Understand basic concepts
- Make initial notes

**Daily Routine:**
- 3-4 hours of reading
- Note-making alongside
- Concept mapping

**Approach:**
1. Start with easy books (O.P. Gauba, Laxmikanth)
2. Build conceptual understanding
3. Don't worry about perfection
4. Focus on syllabus coverage

### Phase 2: Integration (2 months)

**Goals:**
- Link theory with current affairs
- Develop multi-dimensional perspectives
- Start answer writing

**Weekly Routine:**
- 5-6 hours of study
- 10-15 answers per week
- Current affairs integration

**Approach:**
1. Read newspapers daily for IR and Indian Politics
2. Link current events with static concepts
3. Practice writing 10-mark and 20-mark answers
4. Get feedback from mentors

### Phase 3: Revision & Practice (2 months)

**Goals:**
- Comprehensive revision

- 2-3 hours revision
- 5-7 answers daily
- Mock tests weekly

**Approach:**
1. Revise all notes
2. Focus on thinkers and theories
3. Practice diagrams and flowcharts
4. Attempt previous year papers

## Answer Writing Techniques

### Structure for 10-Mark Questions (150 words)

**Introduction** (30 words)
- Define the concept
- Brief context

**Body** (100 words)
- 2-3 main points
- Brief explanation
- Examples if needed

**Conclusion** (20 words)
- Way forward or significance

### Structure for 20-Mark Questions (250 words)

**Introduction** (50 words)
- Define key terms
- Provide context
- Thesis statement

**Body** (180 words)
- 3-4 main arguments
- Multiple perspectives
- Examples and case studies
- Comparative analysis
- Diagram/Flowchart (if applicable)

**Conclusion** (50 words)
- Summarize main points
- Your opinion
- Way forward

## Test Series Strategy

### Types of Tests

1. **Sectional Tests** (Monthly)
   - Political Theory only
   - Indian Politics only
   - Comparative Politics only
   - International Relations only

2. **Paper-wise Tests** (Bi-monthly)
   - Complete Paper I
   - Complete Paper II

3. **Full-Length Mocks** (Last 2 months)
   - Both papers together
   - Time-bound practice

### Evaluation Focus

- **Content**: Accuracy and relevance
- **Structure**: Introduction-Body-Conclusion
- **Presentation**: Diagrams, subheadings
- **Critical analysis**: Depth of analysis
- **Examples**: Relevance and currency
